Elements that led to the start of WW1

Do you know the reason why WW1 started? It didn't just start with killing and shooting did it? Well, no. It had one reason that grew into big consequences.
A group called ''Black hand'' decided to assassinate Arch Duke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the throne of Austria / Hungary. The first attempt
failed when a bomb was thrown to his and his wife's car. The conspirator was arrested. When the car carried on going on down an alternate route,
Gavrilo Princip, part of the black hand members, happened to be there. He shot Franz Ferdinand and his wife Sophie.
This became a crises and Austria declared war on Serbia and in response Russia declared war on Austria. Other
nations obliged by their alliances joined in, bringing the outbreak of the First World War.

Chlorine Gas

Chlorine gas played a major role in WW1. A scientist called Fritz Harbor, experimented in things and made chlorine gas. In April 1915. he sent 150 tons
of this lethal gas to the German troops. The Germans used this to attack the French. First the French thought that it was just a smoke screen but it was
actually gas and it killed them slowly and painfully. But the weather really mattered as well because sometimes the Germans would throw the gas but
instead of going to the French, the wind would push towards them. To solve this problem they pushed the gas out through canons for it to travel further away.
Later The French and British found a way to get rid of this gas and made face masks out of cotton. They later made gas masks to protect them.
