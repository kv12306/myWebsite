World War 2 may have been the biggest and most life changing event recorded in history. This war involved so many countries and thus lead to so many casualties. Many of you may have have heard of Adolf Hitler. He played a very big role in this war.

Elements that led to the start of WW1

Do you know the reason why WW1 started? It didn't just start with killing and shooting did it? Well, no. It had one reason that grew into big consequences.
A group called ''Black hand'' decided to assassinate Arch Duke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the throne of Austria / Hungary. The first attempt failed when a bomb was thrown to his and his wife's car. The conspirator was arrested. When the car carried on going on down an alternate route, Gavrilo Princip, part of the black hand members, happened to be there. He shot Franz Ferdinand and his wife Sophie. This became a crises and Austria declared war on Serbia and in response Russia declared war on Austria. Other nations obliged by their alliances joined in, bringing the outbreak of the First World War.

What was life like on the western front?

On the Western front... Now, what is the western front?

  • The western front is a 400miles + stretch of land that goes through France and Belgium. People who fought on the western front suffered brutally and more the those further ones. The western front was the closest trench line to the enemies and so the bombs and guns were directly pointed towards them. There were 3 trench lines. All the soldiers swapped around them. This is how the life of the people on the western front was.
  • Boredom was a constant problem. Soldiers carried out their chores and tried to catch sleep whenever they could. Soldiers of the front line were very far away from home and wrote letters home to family and they recorded their life in poems and things like that.

  • In the trenches, lice sat inside clothing and caused itchiness and trench fever.
  • Shell shock was a big problem. Because of all the loud sounds of shells and bombs, the soldiers got frequent trauma on the western front.
